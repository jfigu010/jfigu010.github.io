Work Experience

Myself at the Chemistry Laboratory next to a GC (Gas Chromatography) machine

10/20/2016
My time at ISCA Technolgoies, Inc has been one of the greatest in my life. I have learned so much since I started as an intern and I've been able to put on practice the concepts and theories I have learned in school. My journey started as an intern at the end of the Summer 2015. Working part time at the company, I was typical intern that had little bit idea of the fast-paced environment that working in a company demands.
The first weeks/months I was able to learn the ropes as quickly as I could in order to be as helpful as I could every morning I was able to work. With the help of the chemist and my awesome supervisor I was able to understand not only what I was doing in the laboratory but how this impacted the company. I worked mainly on Quality Control and Research and Development as an intern. The studies we hosted and the products we were validating to sell were a main component of the grow that ISCA was having all year 2015.



After my graduation I was able to continue working at the company now full-time as an Analytical Chemist. My work since there has expanded to product development now having the cycle of the creation of the product as a skillset. Being able to participate in all aspects of the product starting with creating a product by Research and Development, EPA registration, product development and Quality Control felt awesome. It open my eyes to my potential in the company. Later as an assignment I was able to help the company save money by fixing an used UV-Spectrophometer which we had bought for cheap with the hope to save money. In no time, I had research the schematic and process of how the equipment worked, planned, tested, and executed a clean up of the system. All these I had to do it on my own time, but in less than two weeks I was able to have the equpiment run smoothly for the purpose we wanted.
I am happy with my job, and professional career that I am starting at ISCA. I can not wait to find out what this path holds for me in the future.
